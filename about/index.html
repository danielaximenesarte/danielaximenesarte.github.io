My little story

Hi, I'm Daniela

Hi, I'm Daniela — and I'm so glad you wandered in. For a long time my days looked nothing like this. I had a steady job, a tidy plan, and a quiet little feeling that something was missing. You might know that feeling too: the one that gently taps you on the shoulder when you least expect it.

I didn't grow up calling myself an artist. I was simply someone with a perfectly sensible career, who kept stealing moments to paint — on lunch breaks, late at night, whenever colour came calling. It took me a while to admit that those stolen moments were the part of the day I loved most.

Taking the leap

So one day, with my heart in my throat, I took the leap. I decided to chase the thing that made me feel most alive — even though I couldn't see the whole staircase, only the very first step.

It was terrifying and wonderful in equal measure. No guarantees, no map. Just a brush, a blank canvas, and a stubborn little hope that following what you love is never really a wrong turn.

Where the magic happens

These days, most pieces begin in a well-loved sketchbook, surrounded by a happy little mess of pastels and a half-finished cup of tea. This is where colours get tested, mistakes get made, and ideas slowly find their feet.

I paint the way I'm learning to live — a little messy, a lot hopeful, always leaving room for a happy accident. Every painting carries a hint of that: a small reminder to keep going, to stay soft, and to notice the tiny good things.

The highs, the lows & everything between

I won't pretend it's all been sunshine. Some days the colours flow and everything sings; on others, nothing works and I wonder what on earth I was thinking. Life, I've found, is exactly like that — a gentle rhythm of highs and lows, and both have something kind to teach us.

But the low days have never once made me wish I'd stayed where I was. If anything, they've made the bright ones brighter, and the whole adventure feel more truly mine.

an open little invitation

Whatever your leap is — take it.

So here's my note to you, friend: you don't have to have it all figured out. You just have to begin. Try the thing. Paint the painting. Send the message. Take the small, brave step toward whatever makes your heart feel a little more alive.

Life is too short — and far too lovely — to spend it wondering “what if.” However big or small your leap, I'm cheering you on, every single step of the way.

With warmth and a paint-stained hand,
Daniela ✿
